The life of Prophet Muhammad (SAW) is filled with lessons of patience, perseverance, compassion, and faith in God. Hereâ€™s a brief story from his life: The Early Years of Prophet Muhammad (SAW) Prophet Muhammad (SAW) was born in Mecca, Saudi Arabia, around the year 570 CE, into the Quraysh tribe. His father, Abdullah, passed away before he was born, and his mother, Amina, died when he was only six years old. Thus, he became an orphan at a young age and was raised by his grandfather, Abdul Muttalib, and later by his uncle, Abu Talib. Despite facing these early hardships, Muhammad grew up to be a kind and trustworthy person, earning the nickname "Al-Amin" (The Trustworthy) due to his honesty and integrity. As he grew older, Muhammad worked as a merchant and was known for his fair dealings. At the age of 25, he was employed by a wealthy widow named Khadijah (RA), who was 15 years older than him. Impressed by his character, she proposed marriage to him, and they were happily married for 25 years. Together, they had children, including their daughter Fatimah (RA), who would become the mother of the Imams of the Prophet's family.

The Revelation When Prophet Muhammad (SAW) was 40 years old, he began spending more time in seclusion, reflecting on the world and seeking answers to the issues he saw around him. He often retreated to a cave called Hira in the mountains surrounding Mecca. It was here, in the month of Ramadan, that he received the first revelation from Allah (SWT) through the angel Jibril (Gabriel). The first words revealed were: "Read in the name of your Lord who created. Created man from a clot. Read, and your Lord is the most generous." (Surah Al-Alaq, 96:1-3) This moment marked the beginning of his prophethood, and the subsequent revelations would continue for 23 years, which were later compiled into the Quran, the holy book of Islam.
